Section 1 — Employment Policies

1. All employees must adhere to company policies and procedures.
2. Employees must maintain professionalism while interacting with colleagues and clients.
3. Employment is subject to verification of documents provided during hiring.
4. Employees must complete onboarding training within the first 30 days.
5. The company follows equal employment opportunity practices.
6. Discrimination based on gender, race, religion, or nationality is strictly prohibited.
7. Employees must maintain confidentiality of company information.
8. Employees must follow company code of conduct at all times.
9. Harassment of any form will not be tolerated in the workplace.
10. Violations of company policy may result in disciplinary action.

Section 2 — Attendance Policy
11. Employees are expected to arrive at work on time.
12. Employees must inform their manager in advance if they are unable to attend work.
13. Repeated late arrivals may lead to disciplinary action.
14. Employees must record their attendance in the official attendance system.
15. Unauthorized absence from work is not permitted.
16. Employees must follow shift schedules assigned by their managers.
17. Remote employees must follow agreed working hours.
18. Attendance records are reviewed monthly by HR.
19. Employees must notify HR for extended absence.
20. Employees must provide justification for any absence longer than one day.

Section 3 — Leave Policy
21. Employees are entitled to 24 annual paid leave as per company policy.
22. Employees must request leave through the HR system.
23. Leave requests should be submitted at least two days in advance.
24. Emergency leave must be communicated to the reporting manager immediately.
25. Sick leave may require a medical certificate.
26. Employees are entitled to take 12 casual leave during the year.
27. Unused leave may be carried forward as per company rules.
28. Managers are responsible for approving leave requests.
29. Leave approval depends on project requirements.
30. Employees must update their leave status in the HR portal.

Section 4 — Workplace Conduct
31. Employees must respect their colleagues and maintain a positive work environment.
32. Offensive language is not permitted in the workplace.
33. Employees must follow company dress code guidelines.
34. Workplace conflicts should be reported to HR.
35. Employees must avoid disruptive behavior during working hours.
36. Employees should maintain cleanliness in the workplace.
37. Personal use of company equipment should be limited.
38. Employees must follow workplace safety guidelines.
39. Smoking is prohibited in designated non-smoking areas.
40. Employees must follow emergency evacuation procedures.

Section 5 — Data Security
41. Employees must protect confidential company data.
42. Sharing company passwords with others is strictly prohibited.
43. Employees must lock their systems when away from their desks.
44. Sensitive information must not be shared outside the organization.
45. Employees must follow IT security guidelines.
46. Unauthorized access to company systems is prohibited.
47. Employees must report security breaches immediately.
48. Company devices must be used only for work purposes.
49. Employees must not install unauthorized software.
50. Data protection policies must be followed by all employees.
Section 6 — Performance Management
51. Employees will receive regular performance evaluations.
52. Managers must provide constructive feedback to employees.
53. Employees should actively participate in performance reviews.
54. Goals should be clearly defined for every employee.
55. Performance metrics must be documented.
56. Training may be recommended for skill improvement.
57. Employees should work towards achieving assigned targets.
58. Performance reviews may influence salary increments.
59. High-performing employees may receive recognition awards.
60. Performance issues may require improvement plans.
Section 7 — Workplace Safety
61. Employees must follow all workplace safety procedures.
62. Hazardous conditions must be reported immediately.
63. Safety equipment must be used where required.
64. Emergency exits must remain accessible.
65. Fire drills may be conducted periodically.
66. Employees must participate in safety training programs.
67. Accidents must be reported to HR immediately.
68. Safety incidents will be investigated by management.
69. Employees must follow building safety regulations.
70. Health and safety policies must be strictly followed.

Section 8 — Remote Work Policy
71. Employees may work remotely with manager approval.
72. Remote employees must maintain productivity standards.
73. Employees must remain available during working hours.
74. Company communication tools must be used for collaboration.
75. Employees must attend scheduled meetings.
76. Internet connectivity is the responsibility of remote employees.
77. Confidential work must be handled securely.
78. Employees must update task progress regularly.
79. Remote work arrangements may be reviewed periodically.
80. Remote employees must follow company data security policies.
Section 9 — Training and Development
81. Employees are encouraged to participate in training programs.
82. Skill development programs may be provided by the company.
83. Employees should complete assigned training modules.
84. Learning resources may be available through company platforms.
85. Managers should support employee development.
86. Employees may request additional training.
87. Certification programs may be sponsored by the company.
88. Continuous learning is encouraged.
89. Training feedback should be provided to HR.
90. Learning outcomes may be evaluated.
Section 10 — Employee Benefits
91. Employees may be eligible for health insurance benefits.
92. Retirement benefits may be provided according to company policy.
93. Paid holidays are provided annually.
94. Employees may receive travel allowances.
95. Performance bonuses may be granted.
96. Employees may receive wellness benefits.
97. Insurance coverage may extend to dependents.
98. Benefits may vary depending on employment level.
99. HR will communicate benefit changes.
100. Employees must review their benefit details annually.
